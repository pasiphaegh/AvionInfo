Waste Management

The garbage room is located on level -1 under the car ramp. Turn right out of the lift, go to the end, then turn right again.

The bin room and red lid bins are designed for small household rubbish only. Bayside Council will fine our building for misuse of the bins. Fines will be passed to offending residents for payment.

Soft plastics and bin liners cannot be recycled. Only place loose recyclable items in the yellow-lid bins. Do not use plastic bin liners or plastic bags.

Parking

Important: Visitor parking spaces are intended for visitors, not residents' vehicles.

Visitor parking is strictly for visitors only and is monitored.

Each apartment has allocated parking spaces and these are registered with Bayside Council under a legal arrangement.

Lifts

The building has two lifts serving all levels. For lift emergencies, please use the emergency intercom in the lift or contact building security.

Emergency Procedures

  • Remain calm and press the emergency button
  • Use the intercom to communicate with security
  • Wait for assistance and do not force doors open

Security & Access

Tailgating

Do not hold the door open for other people, as this poses a security risk. Everyone must have a security fob of their own.

Staircase doors

Close all staircase doors. These are fire safety doors to prevent the spread of smoke and fire and must be kept closed at all times. The doors are also a security point and provide security for residents on each floor, including ground floor.

Locksmiths

For lock and key issues, contact the building manager for approved locksmith details.

Access Fobs

Additional or replacement fobs can be arranged through the building manager.
Replacements cost $150 per fob.

Deliveries

Parcels can be delivered to:

  • Australia Post Parcel lockers (free service; preferred for secure deliveries)
  • Your apartment directly (less secure, as package may be left outside when you are not home)

Building Updates

17 August 2025
Upcoming building repairs

Building Repairs Update We'd like to provide an update and acknowledge that some areas of disrepair have been noticed. The owners corporation has been working for many years to resolve these, and while some issues have already been addressed, others depended on the outcome of a multi-year legal process. That process has now concluded, and the developer is required to carry out rectification works. These works will include replacing all gyprock in the common areas and addressing a number of other building defects. The scale of work means the building will feel like a construction site for a period of time, and some disruption is expected. Once the developer's work is complete, the owners corporation will review any remaining issues and focus on further improvements to enhance the building's appearance and value.

12 July 2025
Bin fines

Bayside Council introduced a new scanning system when emptying bins into their trucks. They will fine residents when the contents of a bin do not match the bin type. For example, when you throw away soft plastics or put plastic bin liners in the yellow lid recycling bins or shoes in the green lid bins.

  • Yellow lid bins = only individual recyclable items. No soft plastics.
  • Green lid bins = only plants or branchs; no soil, rubbish or recycling.

Please pay attention to avoid a fine. Council fines will be passed to offending tenants and owners.
